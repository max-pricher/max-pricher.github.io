STIs & Testing

Knowing your status is the best way to take care of yourself and your partners. Most STIs are treatable, and many are asymptomatic.

The "Big 3" to Watch

  • Chlamydia & Gonorrhea: Often show no symptoms. Easy to cure with antibiotics.
  • Syphilis: Can cause long-term issues if untreated. Look for sores or rashes.

PrEP, PEP & Doxy

Modern medicine offers powerful tools to prevent HIV and other infections.

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

A daily pill (like Truvada or Descovy) or shot that reduces the risk of getting HIV from sex by about 99%.

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)

Emergency medicine taken within 72 hours of a possible HIV exposure. It prevents the virus from taking hold.

DoxyPEP

An antibiotic (doxycycline) taken after sex to prevent bacterial STIs like syphilis, chlamydia, and gonorrhea.

Condoms & Barriers

External Condoms

The standard condom. Effective against fluids and skin-to-skin contact. Use with water or silicone lube only.

Internal Condoms

Can be inserted vaginally or anally before sex. Great for control and coverage.

Lube Guide

Water-Based: Safe for everything.
Silicone: Longer lasting, safe for condoms.
Oil-Based: Breaks condoms! Avoid.
